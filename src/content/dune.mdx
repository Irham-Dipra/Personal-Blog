"I must not fear. Fear is the mind-killer."

Dune is not just a science fiction novel; it's a study in ecology, politics, and religion. Frank Herbert created a universe so rich and textured that reading it feels less like experiencing a story and more like studying a history textbook from the future.

The dry, harsh environment of Arrakis contrasts beautifully with the complex, watery politics of the rest of the universe. 

* **World-building:** 10/10
* **Pacing:** 8/10
* **Impact:** 10/10

Highly recommended for anyone looking to dive deep into a complex narrative.
